The Exceeding Greatness of His Power

After coming to Jesus, you can see, you can know, you can understand, you can discern that which you never understood before. But we all still have a problem. [cite_start]The problem is this: We have the spiritual eyes—they may be open to Jesus and salvation—yet the eyes are closed to much of what is available to us through our Lord Jesus Christ, specifically the power of God. [cite: 63, 64]

And no one comes to Jesus having 20/20 spiritual vision. It's not like you receive Christ and all of a sudden you know everything. Even Jesus, in His humanity—Luke writes, "And Jesus increased in wisdom and stature." [cite_start]He grew in His understanding. [cite: 65-67]

Paul is praying for the church... that your understanding—the spiritual eyes—being enlightened, that you may know what is the hope of His calling. [cite_start]And specifically in verse 19: "And what is the exceeding greatness of His power toward us who believe, according to the working of His mighty power." [cite: 68, 69]

That goes beyond just intellectual processes. That goes beyond memorizing scripture. This is beyond attending church. [cite_start]Paul is praying for revelation and an understanding of what God has already given to you. [cite: 70, 71]

Let's look at verse 19. "What is the exceeding greatness of His power toward us who believe?" That word "exceeding" in the Greek is a compound word: Hyperballo. Hyper means abundance or beyond. Ballo means to throw. [cite_start]So the word exceeding comes from these two Greek words, and it means "to throw beyond the usual mark." [cite: 72-74]

Those of you who love sports, you can relate to this. You know how the field goal kicker knows his maximum distance. But then there is a game, and the pressure is on. His team is behind two points. There are only seconds left on the clock. It's all up to him. The problem is, the goal is about 10 yards beyond what he has ever kicked before. But the ball is snapped, and he comes and he kicks, and the ball goes sailing through the uprights. It's a record for him. It's something he's never done before. It's beyond the mark. [cite_start]That is the idea of this word exceeding. [cite: 75-82]

But that's not enough when describing the power of God toward you. It's exceeding, but the next word, "greatness," in the Greek language is Megathos. That's where we get the word Mega. [cite_start]God's power in you is not just exceeding; it is Mega beyond the mark. [cite: 83-86]

Paul goes on: "according to the working of His mighty power." He's adding word onto word, description onto description. He's building up the power of God. That word "power" literally means "might." So really what it is, is "mighty might." That's the literal translation. [cite_start]It is the Mega, Mighty Might beyond anything we've ever done—the power of God. [cite: 87-90]
